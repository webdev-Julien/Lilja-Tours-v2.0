Departing from Reykjavík, this small-group tour takes you westward along the coast to explore the incredible Snæfellsnes Peninsula, often described as “Iceland in Miniature” for its stunning diversity of landscapes. With a French-speaking guide and a group of no more than 12 participants, you'll enjoy an authentic and enriching experience across one of Iceland’s most captivating regions.

Expect to see lava fields, black sand beaches, volcanic craters, rugged mountains, Icelandic horse herds, seaside villages, and the legendary Snæfellsjökull volcano, famously featured in _Jules Verne’s Journey to the Center of the Earth_.

---

## Tour Overview

- Duration: Approx. 11–12 hours (including pick-up)
- Group Size: Maximum of 12 people
- Guide: Experienced French-speaking guide (from Icelandic Frenchies or partners)
- Departure Points:
  - Hallgrímskirkja, Reykjavík
  - Harpa Concert Hall, Reykjavík
- Frequency: One departure every two weeks
- Minimum Participants: 4 required to confirm departure

> _If the 4-person minimum isn’t met, you may choose to reserve the remaining seats. If more participants join later, you’ll be refunded the difference._

## What You’ll See (Indicative Itinerary):

- Lava fields and volcanic craters
- Icelandic horse farms and remote countryside
- Small fishing villages and coastal harbors
- Black sand beaches and rugged sea cliffs
- The magnificent Snæfellsjökull volcano capped with a glacier — often called _the Icelandic Mount Fuji_

> _Note: The itinerary may change depending on weather and local conditions. Your guide may adjust plans as needed for your safety and enjoyment._
