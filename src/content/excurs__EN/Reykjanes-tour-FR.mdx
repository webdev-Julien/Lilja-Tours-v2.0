Departing from Reykjavík, this small-group tour takes you to the ever-changing Reykjanes Peninsula, a raw and dynamic region shaped by recent volcanic eruptions since 2021. With a French-speaking expert guide and a maximum of 12 participants, you’ll explore an area where lava fields, steaming vents, mud pools, sea cliffs, and birdlife come together in a stunning geological showcase.

The itinerary is adjusted according to volcanic activity, as each eruption reshapes the land and routes, making every tour a one-of-a-kind experience.

---

## Tour Overview

- Duration: 8–10 hours (including pick-up and return)
- Group Size: Maximum of 12 people
- Language: Guided in French
- Transport: Superjeep or minibus depending on conditions
- Departure Points:
  - Hallgrímskirkja, Reykjavík
  - Harpa Concert Hall, Reykjavík
- Frequency: One departure every two weeks
- Minimum Participants: 4 to confirm the tour

> _If the minimum number of guests is not reached, you may choose to reserve the remaining spots. If more participants register later, the difference will be refunded._

---

### What’s Included:

- French-speaking local guide
- Transport by superjeep or minibus
- Pick-up and drop-off in Reykjavík
- Small group experience

## What You’ll See (Indicative Itinerary):

- Recent and ancient lava fields
- Active geothermal zones with bubbling mud pots and steam geysers
- Secluded lakes, rugged sea cliffs, and coastal bird colonies (in season)
- Volcanic landscapes still steaming from recent eruptions

> _Please note: the route may change based on current volcanic conditions. The guide has full discretion to adjust the itinerary for safety and experience quality._

### What to Bring:

- Warm, waterproof clothing
- Sturdy walking shoes

- Camera to capture dramatic terrain
