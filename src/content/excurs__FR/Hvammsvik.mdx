## Discover the Best Hot Springs in the World

Welcome to Hvammsvík Hot Springs, voted the #1 place to visit in the world in 2023 by Timeout. Just a 45-minute drive from Reykjavík, this stunning retreat offers a unique blend of natural hot springs, ocean views, and wildlife encounters. Set on a 1,200-acre private estate, Hvammsvík seamlessly blends modern comfort with ancient Icelandic history, providing an ever-changing and authentic nature experience.

---

## An Ever-Changing Experience

Situated on the edge of the Atlantic Ocean, Hvammsvík’s eight natural hot springs vary in size and temperature, giving visitors a diverse range of soaking experiences. The springs are influenced by the tides, with some pools filling up as they are submerged at high tide, creating a dynamic and ever-changing environment.

## What Makes Hvammsvík Unique?

- Tidal Influence: Some hot springs merge with the ocean at high tide.
- Geothermal and Seawater Fusion: Naturally heated water from 1,400 meters underground blends with fresh seawater, ensuring clean, mineral-rich pools.
- Scenic Serenity: Relax in steaming pools while surrounded by mountains, wildlife, and the open sea.
- Outdoor Changing Facilities: Experience a shower under the open sky.
- Historical Charm: Inspired by Hvammsvík’s heritage, dating back to the 12th century.

---

### Nature and Wildlife

Hvammsvík is not just about hot springs; it’s an immersive nature experience. The estate is home to over 30 species of birds, wild sheep in summer, and, if lucky, a glimpse of an Arctic Fox. Local lore also hints at the presence of elves residing among the rocks.

### Aurora Borealis:

From October to April, the hot springs become a prime spot for witnessing the Northern Lights. Imagine soaking in warm water while the sky dances with green and purple lights—a truly magical experience.

---

### Historical Significance

Hvammsvík has a rich history dating back to the Viking Age, recorded in Landnáma, Iceland’s ancient book of settlers from the 11th century. The area was valued for its natural harbor, geothermal heat, and abundant wildlife, making it a prime location for early inhabitants.
